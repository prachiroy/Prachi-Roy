Odissi:

Odissi, also known as Orissi, is one of the eight classical dance forms of India. It originates from the state of Odisha, in eastern India. It is the oldest surviving dance form of India on the basis of archaeological evidences. The classic treatise of Indian dance, Natya Shastra, refers to it as Odra-Magadhi.I joined the Nataraj School of Dance at the age of 5. My sister did Odissi and I thought it looked cool. I wanted to kill demons and tell Hindu mythology through dance as well. Since then, I have danced for 8 years,learned 8 classical peices, and performed at over 50 events. In a few years I will have my rangapravesh, a solo performance where I perform all hte pieces I have learned and finally become a master of Odissi. The dances I have learned are Mangalacharan, Batu, Sthai, Basant Pallavi, Aravi Pallavi, Mokshya, Jaya Bhagavati, Bagasri Pallavi, and Saveri Pallavi.

Youtube:

VISIT MY YOUTUBE CHANNEL :)

I have a youtube channel where my family in India and Bangladesh can see my dance videos. I post mostly bollywood and bengali folk dances that were choreographed by my sister, Pritha. Many of these performances take place in functions orchestrated by the Bangla Puja Commitee of North America. My closest friends and my family are in the Bangla Puja Commitee of North America, and I often help choreograph dances for their programs. On my channel are bollywood dances such as Chitiya Kalaiyan, Dance Like a Chammiya. Some of my bangla folk dances include Chata Dhoro He Deora, Fagun Haway Haway,Gokul Ki Galiyon Mein. I have even performed at NABC or the North American Bengali Convention. There I performed Baul and Phagun Haway. I have also posted my first odissi performane called Sajini Sajini.
